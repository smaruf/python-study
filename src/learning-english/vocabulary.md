# English Vocabulary and Grammar

## Active and Passive Voice

- **The Active Voice**: The subject performs the action.
- **The Passive Voice**: The subject receives the action (be + past participle).

### Examples

- **Past Simple**
  - Active: He did the task yesterday.
  - Passive: The task was done yesterday.

- **Past Continuous**
  - Active: He was doing the task all day yesterday.
  - Passive: The task was being done all day yesterday.

- **Past Perfect**
  - Active: I realized he had already done the task.
  - Passive: I realized the task had already been done.

- **Past Perfect Continuous**
  - Active: He had been doing the task for 1 hour before he realized it was not necessary.
  - Passive: *[No passive form]*

## Future Tenses

1. I will be driving my car at this time tomorrow.
2. We will be installing the application on all the computers next week.
3. We will have installed the application on all the computers by the end of next week.
4. The application will have been installed by the end of the week.
5. We won't have installed the application on all the computers until next week.

## Phrasal Verbs

- **take forward**: After reviewing the feedback from the beta testing, our team decided to take forward the development of the new software update to enhance user experience.
- **pay off**: My hard work always pays off. (positive results such as satisfaction, money, fulfillment, success, recognition, acknowledgment)

## Passive Voice in Different Tenses

- **Present Simple Passive**: The customer requirements are often discussed.
- **Present Continuous Passive**: Currently, the customer requirements are being discussed.
- **Present Perfect Passive**: The customer requirements have been discussed recently.
- **Past Simple Passive**: The customer requirements were discussed yesterday.
- **Past Continuous Passive**: The customer requirements were being discussed when I joined the meeting.
- **Past Perfect Passive**: When I joined the meeting, I realized that the customer requirements had already been discussed.
- **Future Simple Passive**: I believe the customer requirements will be discussed tomorrow.
- **Modals**: The customer requirements should/have to/need to be discussed tomorrow.
