### **Slide 13: Hedging Fundamentals**
**Title:** "Long Hedge vs Short Hedge"
**Content:**
- **Short Hedge (Producers):** Sell futures to lock selling price
- **Long Hedge (Consumers):** Buy futures to lock purchase price
- **Perfect Hedge:** Basis remains constant
- **Imperfect Hedge:** Basis risk exists but overall risk reduced

**Example:**
- Cotton farmer plants in March, harvests in October
- Sells October cotton futures in March at Tk 5,000/maund
- If spot price falls to Tk 4,500, futures gain offsets spot loss

**Presenter Notes:** Use visual examples with price movements

---

### **Slide 14: Basis Risk Management**
**Title:** "Understanding and Managing Basis Risk"
**Content:**
- **Basis Definition:** Spot Price - Futures Price
- **Basis Patterns:** Convergence to zero at expiry
- **Basis Risk Sources:** Quality differences, location, timing
- **Strengthening Basis:** Spot rises faster than futures
- **Weakening Basis:** Spot falls faster than futures

**Formula:** 
```
Effective Price = Futures Price + Basis at Hedge Lift
Net Hedging Result = (F₂ - F₁) + (S₂ - S₁)
```

**Presenter Notes:** Explain why perfect hedges are rare but hedging still valuable
