## Introduction to Commodity Derivatives
Commodity derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from physical commodities. In Bangladesh, these include futures, options, and spot contracts. They help producers, consumers, and investors manage price risk.

**Key Instruments:**
- Futures
- Options
- Spot
- Margin contracts

---

## Key Concepts & Instruments
### Futures
- Contract to buy/sell a commodity at a future date for a pre-agreed price.
- Can be used for hedging or speculation.

**Diagram: Futures Payoff**
```
Profit

 |      /   
 |     /    
 |____/_____ Price
      Strike
```

### Options
- Right to buy (Call) or sell (Put) a commodity at a specified price.

**Diagram: Option Payoff**
```

 |
```

## Economic Functions
### Price Discovery
- Market determines future commodity prices based on supply & demand.
- Factors: Weather, geopolitics, macroeconomic indicators.

### Risk Management
- Hedging to lock prices and reduce exposure.
- **Example:** Farmer sells futures to secure price for future harvest.

**Benefits:**
- Transparency
- Reduced volatility
- Financial planning

|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Non-Delivery | Commodity lower grade | Financial loss | Certified warehouses, grading systems |
| Transportation | Transit risks | Damage/delay | Cargo insurance, delivery terms |
| Weather/Force Majeure | Natural events | Production disruption | Force majeure clauses, weather derivatives |
| Geopolitical | Political instability | Price spikes | Hedging, monitoring developments |
| Credit | Counterparty defaults | Payment failure | Letters of credit, guarantees |
| Price Volatility | Fluctuations | Gains/losses | Hedging, diversification |

## Hedging Mechanics
**Basis = Spot Price - Futures Price**
- Positive Basis: Spot > Futures (Backwardation)
- Negative Basis: Spot < Futures (Contango)
- Basis risk arises when this relationship changes unexpectedly.

**Long Hedge Example:**
- Gold jewelry order at Tk.130,000/10g.
- Buy gold futures Tk.140,000/10g.
- Final effect locks price, mitigating risk.

**Short Hedge Example:**
- Cotton farmer expects harvest in 4 months.
- Sells futures Tk.3,200/maund at sowing.
- Locks selling price, protects against decline.
